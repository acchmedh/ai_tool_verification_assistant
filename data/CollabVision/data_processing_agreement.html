Data Processing Agreement

Introduction

This Data Processing Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between CollabVision Inc. ("Data Controller") and the user or entity utilizing the CollabVision platform ("Data Processor") in connection with the use of the CollabVision software ("Tool"). The purpose of this Agreement is to establish the terms and conditions under which personal data will be processed by the Data Processor on behalf of the Data Controller, in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and relevant national legislation.

CollabVision is designed to facilitate collaboration among product management teams by providing tools for goal alignment, insight sharing, and progress tracking. As part of this process, certain personal data may be collected, processed, and stored to enable effective platform functionality and user experience. Both parties recognize the importance of safeguarding such data and ensuring its processing complies with all applicable legal requirements.

This Agreement outlines the scope of data processing activities, the obligations of each party, and the technical and organizational measures implemented to protect data integrity and confidentiality. It also details procedures for data breach notifications, data subject rights, and the termination of data processing activities. By using the Tool, the Data Processor agrees to adhere to the terms set forth herein, and the Data Controller commits to providing necessary instructions and support to facilitate lawful and secure data processing.

Purpose of the Agreement

The primary purpose of this Agreement is to define the respective responsibilities and obligations of the Data Controller and the Data Processor concerning the processing of personal data within the scope of the CollabVision platform. This Agreement ensures that all data processing activities are conducted in compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the GDPR, and establishes a clear framework for lawful, transparent, and secure handling of personal data.

Specifically, this Agreement aims to delineate the nature and purpose of data processing, the types of personal data involved, and the categories of data subjects whose data may be processed. It also sets forth the conditions under which data may be collected, used, stored, and transferred, thereby providing assurance to both parties that such activities are performed with appropriate legal bases and safeguards.

Furthermore, the Agreement specifies the responsibilities of the Data Processor in implementing technical and organizational measures to protect personal data from accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access. It also establishes procedures for handling data breaches, data subject rights, and compliance monitoring to ensure ongoing adherence to legal and contractual obligations.

By entering into this Agreement, both parties acknowledge their commitment to maintaining the integrity, confidentiality, and security of personal data processed through the CollabVision platform, thereby fostering trust and accountability in the data processing relationship.

Definitions

For the purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below, unless the context requires otherwise:

  • Data Controller: The natural or legal person who, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data. In this context, CollabVision Inc. acts as the Data Controller.
  • Data Processor: The natural or legal person, entity, or organization that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Data Controller, in accordance with the instructions provided by the Data Controller and for the purposes set forth herein.
  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that is processed through the CollabVision platform, including but not limited to names, email addresses, user activity logs, and other data necessary for platform functionality.
  • Processing: Any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, or destruction.
  • Data Subject: An identified or identifiable natural person to whom the Personal Data relates. Data Subjects include users of the CollabVision platform whose data is processed during use of the Tool.
  • Data Breach: A breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access to Personal Data.
  • Sub-processor: Any third party engaged by the Data Processor to carry out specific processing activities on behalf of the Data Processor, which shall be subject to the same data protection obligations as set forth in this Agreement.

These definitions are intended to ensure clarity and uniform understanding of the key terms used throughout this Agreement and to facilitate compliance with applicable data protection laws.

Roles and Responsibilities

Both the Data Controller and the Data Processor shall adhere to their respective roles and responsibilities as outlined in this Agreement and in accordance with applicable data protection laws. The Data Controller shall be responsible for determining the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data and shall provide clear instructions to the Data Processor regarding the handling of such data. The Data Controller shall also ensure that all data collection and processing activities are lawful and that appropriate lawful bases are identified and documented.

The Data Processor shall process Personal Data only upon documented instructions from the Data Controller unless required by law. The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure the security and confidentiality of Personal Data, including measures to prevent unauthorized access, disclosure, or alteration. The Processor shall assist the Data Controller in fulfilling data subject rights requests and cooperate with supervisory authorities in the event of data breaches or data protection inquiries.

In addition, the Data Processor shall ensure that any personnel engaged in processing are bound by confidentiality obligations and are adequately trained regarding data protection obligations. The Processor shall not engage sub-processors without prior written consent from the Data Controller and shall impose the same data protection obligations on any sub-processor engaged. Both parties shall maintain records of processing activities as required by applicable law and cooperate during audits or inspections to demonstrate compliance with this Agreement and relevant legal requirements.

Failure to fulfill these responsibilities may result in legal liability, sanctions, or damages, and both parties agree to promptly notify each other of any issues that may affect data security or legal compliance.

Data Controller Obligations

The Data Controller shall be responsible for providing clear, lawful, and documented instructions to the Data Processor regarding the processing of Personal Data related to the use of the CollabVision platform. Such instructions shall specify the scope, nature, and purpose of the data processing activities, and shall be consistent with applicable data protection laws and regulations. The Data Controller shall ensure that all collection and processing of Personal Data is based on a valid legal basis, such as consent, contractual necessity, or legitimate interests, and shall maintain records of these legal bases as required by law.

Additionally, the Data Controller shall undertake to inform Data Subjects of the processing activities, including the purpose of processing, data categories, and their rights under applicable law. The Data Controller shall implement appropriate measures to ensure the accuracy, completeness, and timeliness of Personal Data supplied to the Data Processor, and shall update such data as necessary to maintain data quality.

It is also the responsibility of the Data Controller to cooperate with supervisory authorities, respond to data subject requests, and notify the Data Processor of any changes to processing instructions or relevant legal obligations. The Data Controller shall ensure that its personnel involved in data processing activities are adequately trained and aware of their data protection responsibilities. Furthermore, the Data Controller shall notify the Data Processor promptly of any data breach or security incident that may impact Personal Data, enabling appropriate response measures to be undertaken.

Compliance with these obligations is essential to maintain lawful processing and to uphold the rights of Data Subjects, ensuring the integrity and security of Personal Data throughout its lifecycle in connection with the CollabVision platform.

Data Processor Obligations

The Data Processor shall process Personal Data strictly in accordance with the documented instructions provided by the Data Controller and shall not process such data for any purposes outside the scope of this Agreement unless authorized in writing by the Data Controller or as required by applicable law. The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risks associated with the processing activities, including protection against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage to Personal Data.

The Data Processor shall promptly notify the Data Controller of any suspected or actual data breach, security incident, or unauthorized access relating to Personal Data, and shall cooperate fully in investigating, mitigating, and remedying such incidents. The Processor shall maintain comprehensive records of processing activities in accordance with applicable legal requirements and shall make such records available to the Data Controller upon request for audit or review purposes.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall ensure that any personnel engaged in processing are bound by confidentiality obligations and are trained in data protection practices consistent with this Agreement. The Processor shall not engage any sub-processors without prior written consent from the Data Controller and shall impose on any sub-processor the same data protection obligations as those set forth herein. The Data Processor shall remain fully liable for the acts and omissions of any sub-processor engaged in processing Personal Data.

Finally, the Data Processor shall assist the Data Controller in fulfilling data subject rights requests, such as access, rectification, erasure, and data portability, within the applicable legal timeframes. The Processor shall also cooperate with data protection authorities and provide necessary information to demonstrate compliance with data protection obligations under this Agreement and relevant law.

Data Handling and Processing

In connection with the processing of Personal Data under this Agreement, the Data Processor shall only handle data in accordance with the instructions provided by the Data Controller. The Data Processor shall ensure that all processing activities are conducted securely and in compliance with applicable data protection legislation. This includes implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction.

Personal Data collected through the CollabVision platform may include, but is not limited to, user identification information, activity logs, and communication records. The Data Processor shall process such data solely for the purposes specified by the Data Controller, including facilitating platform functionality, enhancing user experience, and ensuring security protocols. Any processing outside these purposes shall require prior written consent from the Data Controller.

Data transfer to sub-processors or third parties shall only occur with the prior approval of the Data Controller and shall be governed by contractual obligations that ensure adequate data protection measures are maintained. The Data Processor shall maintain detailed records of all processing activities, including data transfers and access logs, to demonstrate compliance during audits or inspections.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall ensure timely deletion or anonymization of Personal Data upon the termination of this Agreement or upon instruction from the Data Controller, unless retention is required by law. The Data Processor shall also periodically review its data handling procedures to address emerging security threats and improve data protection practices.

Any ambiguity regarding the scope of permissible processing activities shall be resolved in favor of strict adherence to the Data Controller’s instructions to prevent unauthorized handling of Personal Data.

Data Collection

The Data Controller shall determine the scope and nature of Personal Data to be collected through the CollabVision platform, ensuring that all data collection activities are conducted lawfully and transparently in accordance with applicable data protection regulations. Data collection shall be limited to what is necessary for the purposes explicitly communicated to Data Subjects, and all collection processes shall be carried out with the appropriate legal basis, such as user consent, contractual necessity, or legitimate interests.

In the course of utilizing the platform, the Data Processor shall collect various categories of Personal Data, including but not limited to user identifiers, email addresses, activity logs, and communication records. This data is essential for providing core functionalities, enhancing collaboration, and maintaining platform security. The Data Processor shall implement technical measures to ensure that data collection is secure and that data integrity is preserved during the collection process.

It is the responsibility of the Data Controller to inform Data Subjects regarding the nature and purpose of data collection, as well as their rights under applicable law. The Data Controller shall also ensure that data collection practices are consistent with the initial legal basis and that any additional data collected for new purposes is justified and documented accordingly. Any incidental collection of data outside the scope of this Agreement shall be deemed unauthorized unless explicitly approved in writing by the Data Controller.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall ensure that all collected data is accurate, complete, and up-to-date, and shall take reasonable steps to rectify any inaccuracies upon becoming aware of them. Data collection shall cease immediately if the legal grounds for gathering such data are no longer valid or if instructed otherwise by the Data Controller. All collected data shall be stored securely and retained only for the period necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined herein or as required by law.

Data Storage

The Data Processor shall ensure that all Personal Data processed under this Agreement is stored securely and in accordance with industry best practices and applicable legal requirements. Personal Data shall be stored only for the duration necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Agreement or as mandated by applicable laws. Upon expiry of the retention period or upon termination of this Agreement, the Data Processor shall securely delete or anonymize the relevant data, unless further retention is required by law or explicit instructions from the Data Controller.

The Data Processor shall implement appropriate technical measures to safeguard stored data against unauthorized access, loss, destruction, or alteration. These measures include, but are not limited to, encryption, access controls, regular security assessments, and secure backup procedures. Data shall be stored in controlled facilities with physical security measures to prevent unauthorized physical access, damage, or interference.

In the event of a data breach concerning stored Personal Data, the Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay and shall cooperate fully to mitigate any adverse effects. The Data Processor shall also ensure that any third-party storage providers or hosting services engaged are bound by contractual obligations that impose equivalent data protection responsibilities. Both parties shall maintain detailed records of data storage locations, security measures implemented, and data retention schedules to facilitate compliance audits and legal inquiries.

Any transfer of stored data outside the designated jurisdiction shall only occur in accordance with the provisions outlined in this Agreement and applicable transfer mechanisms, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or adequacy decisions, to ensure the ongoing protection of Personal Data.

Data Transfer

The Data Processor shall not transfer Personal Data to third countries or international organizations unless such transfer is explicitly authorized in writing by the Data Controller and complies with applicable data transfer mechanisms and legal requirements. In cases where transfers are permitted, the Data Processor shall ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, such as Standard Contractual Clauses approved by relevant supervisory authorities or an adequacy decision issued by the relevant data protection authority.

All data transfers shall be documented meticulously, including details of the recipients, transfer dates, and the legal basis for the transfer. The Data Processor shall conduct regular assessments to verify that such transfers are lawful and that the transferred data remains protected in accordance with this Agreement and applicable legislation. The Data Processor shall also notify the Data Controller of any anticipated or actual transfer outside of the agreed scope promptly and shall cooperate to implement necessary measures to ensure compliance.

Furthermore, when engaging sub-processors or third-party service providers involved in data transfer activities, the Data Processor shall ensure that contractual obligations impose equivalent data protection responsibilities, including restrictions on further transfers and requirements for data security. Any transfer not complying with these conditions shall be deemed unauthorized, and the Data Processor shall take immediate steps to rectify the situation, including halting the transfer and notifying the Data Controller. The Data Controller reserves the right to impose additional restrictions or requirements regarding data transfers to safeguard the rights and freedoms of Data Subjects.

Security Measures

The Data Processor shall implement and maintain technical and organizational security measures appropriate to the risks associated with the processing of Personal Data, aiming to protect such data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures shall include, but are not limited to, encryption of data in transit and at rest, access controls based on the principle of least privilege, secure authentication protocols, and regular vulnerability assessments.

The Data Processor shall ensure physical security of the facilities used for data storage, including controlled access, surveillance, and environmental protections to prevent unauthorized physical intrusion or damage. Additionally, the Data Processor shall establish procedures for monitoring, logging, and auditing processing activities to detect and respond to security incidents promptly.

In the event of a suspected or actual data breach, the Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay and cooperate fully to investigate, mitigate, and remediate the breach. The Data Processor shall also keep records of security measures implemented and incidents occurred, to demonstrate compliance with this Agreement and applicable laws. The effectiveness of these security measures shall be reviewed periodically and updated as necessary to address emerging threats and vulnerabilities, ensuring ongoing data protection and compliance with relevant legal requirements.

Technical Safeguards

To ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Personal Data processed under this Agreement, the Data Processor shall implement a comprehensive set of technical safeguards aligned with industry standards and best practices. These safeguards shall include, but are not limited to, encryption of data both in transit and at rest using recognized cryptographic protocols, secure authentication mechanisms such as multi-factor authentication, and role-based access controls to restrict data access solely to authorized personnel.

The Data Processor shall employ regular vulnerability assessments and security testing, including penetration testing where appropriate, to identify and remediate potential security weaknesses in the processing environment. Additionally, the use of intrusion detection and prevention systems shall be mandatory to monitor and respond to suspicious activities in real-time. All systems used for data processing shall be configured to ensure data is processed in a protected environment that prevents unauthorized access or modification.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall maintain secure backup and recovery procedures, ensuring that data can be restored promptly in the event of data loss or system failure. Access to backup data shall be equally protected through encryption and strict access controls. To facilitate incident response, the Data Processor shall establish and regularly update procedures for identifying, reporting, and managing security incidents, including data breaches, in compliance with applicable legal requirements.

Periodic audits of these technical safeguards shall be conducted, and findings shall be documented and addressed to continuously improve the security posture. All measures shall be implemented in accordance with the applicable legal and regulatory frameworks to safeguard Personal Data against evolving threats and ensure compliance with this Agreement and relevant data protection laws.

Organizational Measures

The Data Processor shall establish and maintain comprehensive organizational measures designed to ensure the ongoing confidentiality, integrity, and security of Personal Data processed under this Agreement. These measures shall include, but are not limited to, implementing strict access controls based on role-specific responsibilities, ensuring that only authorized personnel have access to Personal Data necessary for their functions. The Data Processor shall ensure that all personnel involved in data processing are appropriately trained on data protection principles, confidentiality obligations, and the specific security protocols relevant to their roles.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall enforce internal policies and procedures that govern data handling, incident response, and compliance monitoring. Regular training sessions and awareness programs shall be conducted to reinforce data protection responsibilities and keep staff informed of emerging threats and best practices. The organization shall also maintain detailed records of processing activities, including access logs and data handling procedures, to facilitate audits and demonstrate compliance with applicable laws.

In addition, the Data Processor shall implement a process for periodic review and updating of organizational measures to adapt to evolving security risks and regulatory requirements. This includes appointing a designated Data Protection Officer or equivalent responsible for overseeing data protection compliance and ensuring that all organizational practices align with this Agreement and relevant legal standards. All these measures together aim to establish a robust security culture within the organization, minimizing risks associated with data processing activities and ensuring trustworthiness in handling Personal Data.

Audit and Inspection Rights

Both the Data Controller and the Data Processor acknowledge the importance of ensuring compliance with the terms of this Agreement and applicable data protection laws. Accordingly, the Data Controller shall have the right to conduct, or to appoint a qualified third-party auditor to conduct, audits and inspections of the Data Processor’s processing activities, security measures, and compliance procedures relevant to Personal Data processing. Such audits may be performed on reasonable prior notice, during normal business hours, and shall be limited to the scope necessary to verify adherence to contractual obligations and legal requirements.

The Data Processor shall cooperate fully with any such audits by providing access to relevant documentation, records, and facilities, and shall facilitate interviews with personnel involved in data processing activities. The Data Processor shall also maintain detailed records of processing activities, security measures, and incident reports, which shall be made available upon request. The Data Controller reserves the right to conduct inspections at its own expense or to engage third-party auditors, provided that such inspections do not unreasonably disrupt the Data Processor’s operations.

In the event of any identified non-compliance or security vulnerabilities, the Data Processor shall promptly implement corrective actions as directed by the Data Controller. Any findings or deficiencies discovered during an audit shall be documented and addressed within a mutually agreed timeframe. The Data Processor shall also maintain a record of all audits conducted and shall provide summaries or reports upon request. Notwithstanding, the Data Processor’s obligation to cooperate shall be subject to confidentiality and security considerations, and such audits shall not extend beyond the scope necessary to assess compliance with this Agreement and applicable laws.

Audit Process

The audit process shall be initiated upon written request by the Data Controller, who shall provide the Data Processor with reasonable prior notice, not less than thirty (30) days, specifying the scope, objectives, and preferred timing of the audit. The Data Processor shall cooperate fully with the audit by providing access to relevant documentation, processing records, security policies, and any other materials necessary to verify compliance with this Agreement and applicable data protection laws.

During the audit, the Data Processor shall facilitate inspections of physical facilities, review of technical and organizational measures, and interviews with personnel involved in data processing activities. The scope of the audit shall be limited to the areas directly relevant to the processing of Personal Data and the security measures implemented. The Data Processor shall ensure that such inspections do not unreasonably disrupt its operational activities.

Following the completion of the audit, the Data Controller shall provide a written report detailing any identified deficiencies, non-compliance issues, or areas for improvement. The Data Processor shall, within a mutually agreed timeframe, implement corrective actions to address any deficiencies identified during the audit. Both parties shall collaborate in good faith to resolve issues promptly and to improve overall compliance and security posture. All audit findings and corrective measures shall be documented and retained for a minimum period as required by applicable laws and this Agreement.

It is understood that the audit process shall be conducted in accordance with confidentiality obligations, and any information obtained shall be used solely for the purpose of assessing compliance with this Agreement. The Data Controller shall bear the costs associated with the audit unless significant non-compliance or material breaches are discovered, in which case the Data Processor shall bear the costs of remedial actions and follow-up assessments.

Inspection Scope

The scope of inspections conducted under this Agreement shall be limited to the assessment of the Data Processor’s compliance with the contractual obligations, applicable data protection laws, and the technical and organizational security measures implemented to safeguard Personal Data. Such inspections shall encompass a review of processing activities, data security protocols, access controls, incident response procedures, and records of processing activities. The Data Controller or its designated auditor shall specify the particular areas of focus prior to the commencement of any inspection, and these shall be aligned with the purpose of verifying compliance and identifying potential vulnerabilities.

Inspections shall not extend beyond the scope necessary to evaluate adherence to the terms of this Agreement, applicable legal requirements, and the effectiveness of security measures. The Data Processor shall facilitate access to relevant documentation, systems, facilities, and personnel involved in processing activities, ensuring that the inspection process is conducted efficiently and with minimal disruption to ongoing operations. The scope may include, but is not limited to, audits of data storage environments, review of data transfer mechanisms, and evaluations of personnel training and confidentiality measures.

Both parties agree that the inspection scope shall be clearly defined in the audit plan and that any additional areas of review shall require prior mutual consent. Any findings or deficiencies identified during the inspection shall be documented, and the Data Processor shall undertake corrective actions within a reasonable timeframe. The scope of the inspection shall be periodically reviewed and adjusted as necessary to reflect changes in processing activities, emerging security threats, or legal obligations, ensuring continuous compliance and data protection.

Subprocessors

The Data Processor shall not engage any sub-processor to carry out any processing activities involving Personal Data without the prior written consent of the Data Controller. Any such engagement shall be subject to a written agreement that imposes data protection obligations equivalent to those set forth in this Agreement, ensuring the security and confidentiality of the Personal Data processed. The Data Processor shall maintain a current list of all approved sub-processors and shall make this list available to the Data Controller upon request.

In the event the Data Processor intends to appoint a new sub-processor, it shall notify the Data Controller in writing at least thirty (30) days prior to such appointment, providing sufficient details regarding the sub-processor’s identity, processing activities, and the safeguards in place. The Data Controller shall have the right to object to the proposed sub-processor within this period. If the Data Controller objects to the appointment, the Data Processor shall not engage the sub-processor and shall seek an alternative arrangement that complies with this Agreement.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall ensure that any sub-processor or third-party engaged in processing Personal Data adheres to the same data protection obligations as those described in this Agreement, including confidentiality, security measures, and data processing limitations. The Data Processor shall remain fully liable for any acts or omissions of its sub-processors that cause a breach of this Agreement or applicable data protection laws. This clause shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

Such objections must be communicated in writing within the notice period, and if the Data Controller objects, the Data Processor shall not proceed with the engagement unless alternative arrangements are made that meet the security and data protection standards outlined in this Agreement.

All subprocessors engaged by the Data Processor are contractually bound to comply with data protection obligations that are at least equivalent to those set forth herein. The Data Processor shall retain responsibility for any breaches or non-compliance attributable to its subprocessors and shall ensure that their processing activities are conducted in accordance with this Agreement and applicable laws.

Subprocessor Obligations

Each sub-processor engaged by the Data Processor shall be subject to contractual obligations that impose data protection standards at least equivalent to those set forth in this Agreement. Such obligations shall include, but are not limited to, implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures to safeguard Personal Data, restricting processing activities to the instructions provided by the Data Processor and, by extension, the Data Controller, and ensuring confidentiality obligations are imposed on all personnel involved in processing activities.

Sub-processors shall cooperate fully with the Data Processor and the Data Controller in fulfilling data subject rights requests, conducting audits, and responding to data breaches or security incidents. The Data Processor shall ensure that any sub-processor is bound by written agreements that explicitly prohibit further engagement or transfer of Personal Data without prior written approval from the Data Processor and, where applicable, the Data Controller. Additionally, sub-processors shall notify the Data Processor promptly of any security vulnerabilities or breaches affecting Personal Data they process.

The Data Processor shall remain fully liable for any acts or omissions of its sub-processors that result in non-compliance with this Agreement or applicable data protection laws. In the event of a breach attributable to a sub-processor, the Data Processor shall take immediate corrective action and ensure that remedial measures are implemented without delay. Furthermore, the Data Processor shall maintain a current list of all sub-processors and shall ensure that contractual arrangements are in place prior to engaging new sub-processors, thereby maintaining compliance and data security standards throughout the processing lifecycle.

Data Subject Rights

Both the Data Controller and the Data Processor shall ensure that Data Subjects are able to exercise their rights under applicable data protection laws, including but not limited to the GDPR. The Data Controller shall be primarily responsible for facilitating and responding to Data Subject requests related to access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection to processing.

The Data Processor shall assist the Data Controller in fulfilling these obligations by implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures to confirm the identity of Data Subjects, process requests within legally mandated timeframes, and provide necessary information or documentation to support Data Subject rights. Such assistance may include providing access logs, processing histories, and ensuring secure communication channels for Data Subject inquiries.

In the event a Data Subject exercises their rights, the Data Controller shall notify the Data Processor promptly and provide clear instructions for responding to the request. The Data Processor shall process the request strictly in accordance with such instructions and applicable legal requirements. The Data Processor shall also maintain records of all Data Subject requests and the actions undertaken, which shall be made available to the Data Controller upon request.

Both parties agree to cooperate fully to ensure that Data Subjects’ rights are respected and protected throughout the processing lifecycle. Any refusal to comply with Data Subject requests must be justified under applicable law, and the Data Controller shall inform the Data Subject accordingly, with the Data Processor providing support as needed to facilitate such communication.

Access Requests

Both the Data Controller and the Data Processor acknowledge that Data Subjects have the right to request access to their Personal Data in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the GDPR. The Data Processor shall implement procedures to facilitate timely and accurate responses to such requests, ensuring compliance with statutory timeframes, typically within one (1) month of receipt of the request. The Data Processor shall verify the identity of the Data Subject prior to releasing any Personal Data to prevent unauthorized disclosures.

To process an access request, the Data Subject shall submit a written request to the Data Controller, who shall, in turn, notify the Data Processor of the request and provide necessary instructions. The Data Processor shall cooperate fully by providing all relevant data, processing logs, and documentation necessary to substantiate the Data Subject’s entitlement, while maintaining the confidentiality and security of the data involved. In cases where the requested Personal Data is stored across multiple systems or locations, the Data Processor shall aggregate and compile the data to produce a comprehensive response.

If the Data Subject’s request is deemed unfounded or excessive, the Data Processor reserves the right to request additional information or clarification from the Data Subject or the Data Controller to appropriately assess the validity of the request. The Data Processor shall not unreasonably refuse or delay responses and shall document all actions taken in relation to each request. Both parties agree to cooperate in good faith to ensure Data Subjects’ rights are upheld and that responses are provided in a manner consistent with legal requirements and organizational policies.

Rectification and Erasure

Both the Data Controller and the Data Processor shall facilitate the rectification and erasure of Personal Data in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the GDPR. The Data Subject shall have the right to request the correction of inaccurate or incomplete data, and the Data Processor shall ensure that such requests are processed promptly and efficiently. Upon receipt of a valid rectification request, the Data Processor shall amend the relevant data within its systems and notify the Data Controller of the changes made.

Similarly, Data Subjects shall have the right to request the erasure of their Personal Data, subject to legal obligations or legitimate reasons for retention. The Data Processor shall implement erasure procedures to delete or anonymize Personal Data upon instruction from the Data Controller or when the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected. If the data cannot be deleted due to legal retention requirements, the Data Processor shall restrict access and mark the data for eventual deletion once compliance obligations are fulfilled.

In carrying out rectification or erasure requests, the Data Processor shall verify the identity of the Data Subject to prevent unauthorized alterations or deletions. All such actions shall be documented, including the date, scope, and nature of the changes. The Data Processor shall cooperate with the Data Controller to ensure that all relevant systems and backups are updated accordingly, and shall notify the Data Controller of any difficulties encountered in fulfilling such requests. Both parties shall maintain records of all rectification and erasure activities to demonstrate compliance with applicable legal standards and this Agreement.

Term and Termination

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until terminated by either party in accordance with the provisions set forth herein. Either party may terminate this Agreement for any reason by providing written notice to the other party at least thirty (30) days prior to the intended date of termination. Upon termination, the Data Processor shall, subject to the instructions of the Data Controller, securely delete or anonymize all Personal Data processed under this Agreement, unless applicable law requires retention of such data for a specified period.

In the event of a material breach of this Agreement by either party, the non-breaching party shall have the right to terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the breach remains unremedied within fifteen (15) days of receipt of such notice. Termination shall not affect any rights or obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination. The Data Processor shall cooperate with the Data Controller to ensure a smooth transition and to facilitate the secure transfer or deletion of Personal Data.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, the obligations concerning data confidentiality, security, and the return or deletion of Personal Data shall survive termination of this Agreement. Termination shall also be without prejudice to any rights or remedies available to either party under applicable law or in equity. The parties agree to settle all outstanding obligations and complete any ongoing processing activities prior to the effective date of termination to ensure compliance with data protection standards and contractual commitments.

Agreement Duration

The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date specified herein and shall continue in effect for an initial period of twelve (12) months, unless terminated earlier in accordance with the provisions set forth herein. Upon the expiration of the initial term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days prior to the expiration of the then-current term. Such renewal shall be contingent upon the continued compliance by both parties with all applicable legal and contractual obligations pertaining to data processing and security.

Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time for convenience by providing written notice to the other party no less than thirty (30) days prior to the intended termination date. Termination for cause may be effected immediately if either party breaches material obligations under this Agreement and such breach remains unremedied for a period of fifteen (15) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach. In the event of termination, the Data Processor shall, in accordance with the Data Controller’s instructions, securely delete or return all Personal Data processed under this Agreement, unless retention is mandated by applicable law.

Notwithstanding any termination, the obligations concerning confidentiality, data security, and the handling of Personal Data shall survive for a period of five (5) years from the date of termination or until such data is fully deleted or anonymized, as applicable. Both parties agree to cooperate in good faith to ensure a seamless transition and to fulfill all outstanding responsibilities related to data processing and compliance during and after the termination process.

Termination Conditions

The termination of this Agreement shall be subject to the conditions outlined herein and shall only be effected in accordance with the procedures specified. Either party may initiate termination if the other party materially breaches any obligation under this Agreement and fails to remedy such breach within fifteen (15) days after receipt of written notice specifying the nature of the breach. Additionally, termination may occur if either party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or is subject to any legal or regulatory action that materially affects its ability to fulfill its contractual obligations.

Prior to termination, the terminating party shall provide written notice to the other party, specifying the effective date of termination, which shall not be less than thirty (30) days from the date of such notice unless immediate termination is justified due to material breach or legal necessity.
