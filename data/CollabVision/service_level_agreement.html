Service Level Agreement for CollabVision

Introduction

This Service Level Agreement ("SLA") delineates the terms and conditions under which CollabVision ("Service Provider") shall deliver its collaborative platform to its clients, primarily targeting product management teams seeking to optimize their workflows. This document is intended to establish clear expectations regarding service performance, support obligations, and responsibilities of both parties, thereby ensuring a mutual understanding of the scope and limitations of the services provided.

By utilizing CollabVision, the client acknowledges and agrees to adhere to the terms set forth herein, including any amendments or updates that may be issued from time to time. The SLA applies to all users authorized by the client to access and use the platform, and it forms an integral part of the overarching contractual relationship between the Service Provider and the client.

The purpose of this SLA is to define the service levels that the Service Provider commits to maintain, including system availability, incident response times, and support procedures. It also specifies the metrics used to measure service performance, as well as the escalation pathways for resolving issues that may impact the client's operations. This agreement aims to foster a transparent, reliable, and efficient service environment, thereby enabling product management teams to achieve their organizational objectives with confidence.

Purpose of the Agreement

The primary purpose of this Agreement is to establish a comprehensive understanding between the Service Provider and the client regarding the scope, quality, and delivery of the CollabVision platform services. This document aims to define the rights, responsibilities, and obligations of each party to ensure a mutually beneficial and legally enforceable relationship. By articulating specific service commitments, performance standards, and support protocols, the Agreement seeks to minimize ambiguities and set clear expectations for service delivery.

Furthermore, the Agreement delineates the procedures for reporting, escalating, and resolving issues that may arise during the course of service provision. It ensures that both parties are aligned on incident management processes, communication channels, and escalation pathways, thereby facilitating prompt resolution of service disruptions or deficiencies. The Agreement also outlines the terms related to data security, confidentiality, and compliance with applicable laws and regulations, which are critical to protecting the interests of both the client and the Service Provider.

In addition, this Agreement serves to formalize the terms under which updates, maintenance, and enhancements to the platform will be delivered. It establishes the framework for ongoing collaboration, performance monitoring, and continuous improvement efforts. Ultimately, the purpose of this Agreement is to foster a sustainable, transparent, and efficient partnership that supports the client’s objectives while safeguarding the integrity and reliability of the CollabVision platform.

Definitions

For the purposes of this Service Level Agreement ("SLA" or "Agreement"), the following terms shall have the meanings specified below, unless the context requires otherwise:

  • "CollabVision" or "Service Provider" refers to the enterprise software platform and related services provided by the Service Provider, designed to facilitate collaborative product management workflows.
  • "Client" means the individual or entity authorized by the client organization to access and use the CollabVision platform pursuant to this Agreement.
  • "User(s)" refers to all individuals within the Client organization who are granted access to the platform, including but not limited to product managers, team members, and authorized administrators.
  • "Service Levels" denote the agreed-upon standards of system availability, performance, incident response, and support described in this SLA.
  • "System Availability" indicates the proportion of time the platform is operational and accessible to authorized Users, measured in percentage terms over a specified period.
  • "Incident" means any event that disrupts or degrades the normal functioning of the platform, including system outages, bugs, or security breaches.
  • "Support Services" encompass the technical assistance, troubleshooting, and issue resolution provided by the Service Provider to the Client and Users.
  • "Downtime" refers to periods when the platform is unavailable or non-functional due to scheduled maintenance or unplanned outages.
  • "Response Time" indicates the elapsed time between the Client’s reporting of an incident and the Service Provider’s initial response to such incident.
  • "Maintenance" includes scheduled updates, patches, and system improvements performed by the Service Provider to ensure optimal platform performance and security.

All definitions herein are intended to provide clarity and consistency throughout this Agreement. In the event of any inconsistency or ambiguity, the terms as defined in this section shall prevail unless explicitly stated otherwise in the context of the Agreement.

In the event of an unplanned outage or incident that materially affects the platform’s performance, the Service Provider shall respond in accordance with the incident response procedures outlined in this SLA. Response times will be classified based on the severity level of the incident, with critical issues receiving initial response within one hour and less severe issues within four hours. Performance metrics shall be monitored continuously, and the Service Provider shall maintain detailed logs and reports to verify compliance with the agreed-upon service levels. Furthermore, the Service Provider shall not be held liable for downtime caused by force majeure events, including natural disasters, governmental actions, or significant network failures beyond its control.

Failure to meet the specified availability thresholds for two consecutive months shall constitute a material breach of this SLA, entitling the Client to appropriate remedies, including service credits or other compensation as detailed in the applicable support and compensation clauses. The Service Provider reserves the right to adjust maintenance schedules or performance standards upon mutual written agreement to accommodate evolving technical or operational requirements.

The Service Provider provides an uptime guarantee of 99.9% for the CollabVision platform, measured on a monthly basis, excluding scheduled maintenance periods and circumstances classified as force majeure. The Service Provider shall conduct an investigation into the incident and determine whether the uptime shortfall resulted from the Service Provider’s failure or external factors beyond its reasonable control. It is expressly understood that the guarantee does not apply to downtime caused by scheduled maintenance, client-side configurations, third-party integrations, or events classified as force majeure, including natural disasters, government actions, or network failures outside the control of the Service Provider.

In cases where the Service Provider acknowledges a breach of the uptime guarantee, the remedy shall be limited to the issuance of service credits equivalent to a percentage of the monthly service fee, calculated proportionally based on the duration of the outage and the severity of the impact. The Service Provider reserves the right to modify or update the uptime guarantee upon mutual written agreement, provided that any such modifications are communicated in advance and documented accordingly.
