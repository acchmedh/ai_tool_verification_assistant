Data Processing Agreement

Introduction

This Data Processing Agreement (hereinafter referred to as the "Agreement") is entered into by and between the Data Controller and the Data Processor (collectively referred to as the "Parties") to establish the terms and conditions under which personal data will be processed in connection with the use of CollabCraft Pro ("the Tool"). This Agreement is intended to ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other relevant legislation, and to define the responsibilities and obligations of each Party regarding data processing activities.

The purpose of this Agreement is to outline the scope of data processing, specify the nature and purpose of processing, and establish the security measures necessary to protect personal data. CollabCraft Pro is designed to facilitate collaboration among product managers by enabling efficient communication, goal alignment, and workflow management. As part of the operation of the Tool, certain personal data may be processed, including but not limited to user identifiers, project-related information, and communication records.

By entering into this Agreement, the Parties acknowledge their respective roles and responsibilities concerning data processing activities. The Data Controller retains authority over the purposes and means of processing personal data, while the Data Processor agrees to process data only on documented instructions from the Controller and to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to safeguard the data. This Agreement shall remain in effect for the duration of the data processing activities unless terminated in accordance with its terms.

Purpose of the Agreement

The purpose of this Agreement is to define the legal framework governing the processing of personal data by the Data Processor on behalf of the Data Controller in relation to the use of CollabCraft Pro. It establishes the respective obligations, responsibilities, and liabilities of each Party to ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the GDPR and any other relevant jurisdictional legislation.

Specifically, this Agreement aims to articulate the scope and nature of data processing activities, including the types of personal data involved, the purposes for which such data is processed, and the duration of processing. It also delineates the technical and organizational security measures that the Data Processor shall implement to protect personal data from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

Furthermore, this Agreement ensures that the Data Processor processes personal data strictly in accordance with documented instructions from the Data Controller, and only for the purposes explicitly authorized in relation to the operation and maintenance of CollabCraft Pro. The Parties agree that any deviation from these instructions shall constitute a breach of this Agreement and may result in contractual remedies, including termination or indemnification.

By establishing these parameters, the Agreement seeks to promote transparency, accountability, and lawful processing practices, thereby safeguarding the rights of data subjects and ensuring that all data processing activities are conducted in a manner that complies with applicable legal requirements.

Definitions

For the purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below unless the context indicates otherwise:

  • Data Controller: The natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data. In the context of this Agreement, the Data Controller is responsible for defining the objectives and scope of data collection and processing related to the use of CollabCraft Pro.
  • Data Processor: The natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which processes personal data on behalf of the Data Controller. The Data Processor shall act only on documented instructions from the Data Controller and shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure data security.
  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ('Data Subject') that is processed under this Agreement. Personal Data includes, but is not limited to, user identifiers, project data, communication records, and other related information.
  • Processing: Any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, alignment, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.
  • Data Subject: An identified or identifiable natural person to whom the Personal Data relates. Data Subjects may include users of CollabCraft Pro or other individuals whose data is processed within the scope of this Agreement.
  • Services: The activities and functionalities provided by CollabCraft Pro that enable collaboration, communication, and workflow management for product managers, as specified in the Tool's purpose.
  • Security Measures: The technical and organizational measures implemented by the Data Processor to protect Personal Data against unauthorized access, accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, disclosure, or misuse.

Terms not expressly defined herein shall have the meanings ascribed to them under applicable data protection laws and regulations. The singular includes the plural and vice versa, as the context requires.

Scope and Applicability

This Agreement applies to all processing activities involving Personal Data undertaken by the Data Processor in connection with the use of CollabCraft Pro, regardless of the geographic location of the Data Subject or the Parties, unless explicitly excluded by applicable law. It governs the processing of Personal Data from the point of collection, through storage and use, to eventual deletion or anonymization, in accordance with the instructions provided by the Data Controller.

The obligations set forth herein extend to all personnel, affiliates, and subcontractors engaged by the Data Processor who have access to Personal Data processed within the scope of CollabCraft Pro. The Parties acknowledge that the Data Controller retains ultimate authority over the purposes and means of data processing, and the Data Processor shall act solely on the documented instructions of the Controller, except where required by law. Any processing activities outside the scope of this Agreement shall require prior written approval from the Data Controller.

This Agreement is applicable during the duration of the contractual relationship between the Parties concerning the use of CollabCraft Pro and shall remain in effect until all Personal Data has been securely deleted or anonymized in accordance with the terms herein. The scope also covers any updates, modifications, or enhancements to the Tool that involve processing of Personal Data, ensuring that all such activities comply with the provisions of this Agreement.

In the event of conflicts between this Agreement and other contractual documents, the provisions herein shall prevail with respect to data processing activities. The Parties agree to cooperate in good faith to ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws and to address any issues arising from the scope of processing activities under this Agreement.

Parties Involved

The Data Controller is the entity that determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data related to the use of CollabCraft Pro. The Data Processor shall process Personal Data only within the scope of these instructions, and any deviations require prior written approval from the Data Controller. Both Parties agree to comply with applicable data protection laws and regulations relevant to their respective roles. The Data Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure the security and confidentiality of the Personal Data processed on behalf of the Data Controller. Furthermore, the Parties acknowledge that the roles may overlap or be shared in certain circumstances, but for the purposes of this Agreement, their responsibilities shall be clearly delineated.

Covered Data Types

This section delineates the specific categories of Personal Data that may be processed under this Agreement in connection with the use of CollabCraft Pro. The Data Processor shall only handle and process data types explicitly identified within this scope and shall ensure that processing activities remain confined to the purposes outlined herein.

The Personal Data covered by this Agreement includes, but is not limited to, user identifiers such as usernames, email addresses, and access credentials; project-related information including project names, descriptions, deadlines, and status updates; communication records such as messages, comments, and activity logs; and other related data generated or collected during the course of utilizing CollabCraft Pro. Sensitive data, such as health information or financial details, shall only be processed if explicitly authorized and in compliance with applicable laws.

The scope of data processed may also encompass metadata related to user interactions, device information, IP addresses, and browser types, which are collected automatically to facilitate system security and improve service quality. All such data shall be processed in accordance with this Agreement and only for the purposes of providing, maintaining, and improving the functionalities of CollabCraft Pro.

It is acknowledged that the precise types of Personal Data processed may vary depending on user activities and configurations within the Tool. The Data Processor shall maintain a record of all data types processed and shall inform the Data Controller promptly of any changes that may impact data handling practices or legal compliance. Any processing of data outside the scope defined herein shall require prior written approval from the Data Controller to ensure continued adherence to applicable data protection requirements.

Responsibilities of the Parties

The Controller shall ensure that all instructions comply with applicable data protection laws and that the Personal Data provided for processing is accurate, complete, and lawful. Furthermore, the Controller shall establish and communicate any specific requirements for data security, access controls, and retention policies to the Data Processor.

The Processor shall also maintain records of processing activities, facilitate audits, and cooperate fully with data protection authorities or other authorized entities upon request.

In the event of a data breach or security incident involving Personal Data, the Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay and cooperate fully to mitigate any potential harm. The Parties shall jointly develop and maintain procedures for responding to data breaches, including notification timelines and remedial actions, to ensure compliance with applicable law and minimize risks to Data Subjects.

The Data Controller shall bear primary responsibility for defining the purposes and legal basis for the processing of Personal Data in connection with the use of CollabCraft Pro. The Controller shall ensure that all instructions provided to the Data Processor are lawful, clear, and specific, aligning with applicable data protection laws, including GDPR requirements. Finally, the Data Controller shall be responsible for establishing and enforcing internal policies related to data security, access control, and data retention, and shall ensure that personnel involved in data processing activities are adequately trained and aware of their legal obligations. The Controller shall also notify the Data Processor of any known or suspected violations of data security that could impact the Personal Data processed under this Agreement.

The Data Processor shall carry out processing activities strictly in accordance with the documented instructions provided by the Data Controller and shall not process Personal Data for any purposes outside the scope of this Agreement. The Processor shall cooperate with the Data Controller and relevant authorities in investigating and resolving such incidents. The Processor shall ensure that such deletion or return is carried out securely and in accordance with the instructions of the Data Controller.

In addition, the Data Processor shall establish procedures for incident detection, reporting, and response, ensuring that any security breaches involving Personal Data are promptly identified and addressed. The breach notification process shall be initiated without undue delay and in accordance with applicable laws, providing sufficient detail to enable effective mitigation. All security measures shall be reviewed and updated periodically to adapt to evolving threats and technological advancements, with documentation maintained to substantiate compliance with this section.

The Data Processor shall also ensure that all personnel involved in processing are adequately trained on data security protocols and are contractually bound to uphold confidentiality obligations. Any subcontractors or third-party service providers engaged shall also adhere to equivalent security standards, confirmed through appropriate contractual provisions and audit rights.

Technical Safeguards

The Data Processor shall employ robust technical safeguards to ensure the security and confidentiality of Personal Data processed under this Agreement. These safeguards shall include, but are not limited to, encryption protocols for data at rest and in transit, multi-factor authentication mechanisms, and secure access controls that restrict data access solely to authorized personnel. The encryption methods used shall comply with industry standards such as AES-256 or equivalent, ensuring that data remains protected against unauthorized interception or disclosure.

The Data Processor shall also implement regular vulnerability assessments, including penetration testing and security audits, to identify and remediate potential vulnerabilities within its systems and infrastructure. These assessments shall be conducted at intervals consistent with industry best practices and legal requirements, with results documented and reviewed by qualified security personnel.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall maintain detailed audit logs of all access and processing activities related to Personal Data. These logs shall be retained for a period sufficient to enable effective investigation and compliance monitoring, and shall be protected against alteration or unauthorized access. In the event of a security incident or breach, the Data Processor shall utilize these logs to facilitate incident response and forensic analysis.

All technical safeguards shall be reviewed and updated periodically to ensure continued effectiveness against emerging threats. The Data Processor shall also ensure that any subcontractors or third-party providers engaged to process Personal Data adhere to equivalent security standards, as established through contractual obligations and audit rights. Adequate training shall be provided to relevant personnel to maintain awareness of security protocols and best practices, thereby supporting the overall integrity and security of the data processing environment.

By implementing these organizational measures, the Data Processor aims to foster a culture of data protection compliance and minimize risks associated with personal data processing activities.

Incident Response Protocols

In the event of a security incident or data breach involving Personal Data, the Data Processor shall activate the Incident Response Protocols outlined herein to ensure prompt, effective, and compliant remediation. The Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay, and in any case within twenty-four (24) hours of becoming aware of the incident, unless otherwise specified by applicable law. The notification shall include a detailed description of the nature of the breach, the types and number of Data Subjects affected, the potential risks involved, and the measures taken or planned to mitigate the impact.

The Data Processor shall cooperate fully with the Data Controller to investigate the incident, including providing access to relevant logs, systems, and personnel as necessary. Additionally, the Processor shall assist in assessing the scope of the breach and determining whether notification obligations to Data Subjects or supervisory authorities are triggered under applicable laws. If notification to Data Subjects is required, the Data Processor shall follow the instructions provided by the Data Controller to ensure that such communication is clear, accurate, and timely.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall review and enhance existing security measures to prevent recurrence of similar incidents. All corrective actions, including system updates, process improvements, and staff training, shall be documented and implemented promptly. The Parties shall also conduct a post-incident review to evaluate response effectiveness and identify areas for improvement. It is understood that the Incident Response Protocols shall be periodically reviewed and updated to adapt to evolving threats and regulatory requirements, ensuring ongoing preparedness and compliance.

Data Subject Rights

The Parties acknowledge and shall facilitate the exercise of Data Subjects’ rights in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the GDPR. Data Subjects shall have the right to access their Personal Data processed under this Agreement, to rectify inaccurate or incomplete information, and to request the erasure of their data where applicable. Furthermore, Data Subjects shall have the right to restrict processing, to object to processing on legitimate grounds, and to data portability where such rights are recognized under relevant legislation.

The Data Processor shall implement procedures to respond to Data Subject requests promptly and efficiently, ensuring that such requests are addressed within the statutory timeframes, typically within one (1) month of receipt. The Processor shall verify the identity of the Data Subject prior to providing access or making amendments to Personal Data. In cases where requests are complex or require additional verification, the response period may be extended in accordance with legal requirements, provided the Data Subject is informed accordingly.

All such requests and the corresponding responses shall be documented and retained by the Parties for accountability purposes. The Data Controller shall inform the Data Processor of any Data Subject requests received directly and shall coordinate responses to ensure compliance with applicable laws. The Parties agree to cooperate fully to uphold Data Subjects’ rights, including assisting each other in fulfilling any legal obligations related to data access, correction, or deletion, and maintaining transparency regarding processing activities.

Failure to comply with Data Subject rights may result in legal sanctions or penalties under applicable legislation. Therefore, each Party shall ensure that its personnel are adequately trained and that internal policies are in place to support the timely and lawful handling of Data Subject requests.

Access and Rectification Requests

In accordance with applicable data protection laws, the Parties shall establish and maintain clear procedures for handling Data Subject requests for access and rectification of their Personal Data. The Data Controller shall be responsible for receiving and verifying the legitimacy of such requests, ensuring that they are processed in a timely manner, generally within one (1) month from receipt, unless an extension is justified by the complexity or volume of requests.

The Data Processor shall assist the Data Controller by providing all necessary information and support to facilitate the verification of the Data Subject’s identity and to fulfill the request. Upon receiving a valid request, the Data Processor shall promptly retrieve the relevant Personal Data from its records and provide the Data Subject with a copy in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. The Data Processor shall also implement procedures to ensure that any updates or corrections requested by Data Subjects are accurately and securely applied to the Personal Data held.

All requests and responses shall be documented and retained for accountability and audit purposes. If a request is manifestly unfounded or excessive, particularly because of its repetitive nature, the Data Processor may charge a reasonable fee or refuse to act upon the request, provided that the Data Subject is informed of the basis for such refusal. In all cases, the Parties shall cooperate to ensure compliance with applicable legal obligations and to safeguard the Data Subjects' rights.

It is essential that both Parties establish internal protocols for prompt communication and resolution of access and rectification requests, including designated points of contact. This collaborative approach is intended to ensure transparency, accountability, and the lawful exercise of Data Subjects’ rights throughout the duration of the data processing activities under this Agreement.

Erasure and Data Portability

In accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the GDPR, the Data Processor shall facilitate the erasure of Personal Data upon the termination of this Agreement or upon the Data Controller’s instruction, unless otherwise required by law. The Processor shall securely delete or anonymize Personal Data in a manner that ensures it cannot be reconstructed or retrieved, and shall provide written confirmation of such deletion upon request. The Data Controller remains responsible for verifying that deletion has been completed in compliance with legal and contractual obligations.

Furthermore, Data Subjects shall have the right to obtain a copy of their Personal Data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, commonly referred to as data portability. The Data Processor shall, upon request, provide such data to the Data Subject or directly to another data controller designated by the Data Subject, where technically feasible. The provision of data shall be executed in a timely manner, generally within thirty (30) days of receipt of the request, and shall encompass all relevant Personal Data processed under this Agreement.

All data portability requests shall be documented and responded to with due diligence to ensure compliance with legal standards. The Data Processor shall implement appropriate measures to ensure that the transfer of Personal Data does not compromise security or data integrity. Any transfer shall be conducted in accordance with the instructions of the Data Controller, and where applicable, with the consent of the Data Subject. The Parties shall cooperate to meet statutory deadlines and to maintain transparency and accountability throughout the process.
