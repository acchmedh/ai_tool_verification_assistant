Neither Tool_5 nor the User or Customer shall be held liable for any failure or delay in performing their obligations under this Agreement if such failure or delay results from causes beyond their reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, labor disputes, governmental actions, pandemics, or interruptions in telecommunications or internet services.

In the event of a force majeure event, the affected party shall promptly notify the other party in writing, detailing the nature of the event and the expected duration of the disruption. However, the term “promptly” is not specifically defined within this Agreement, which may result in ambiguity regarding the timing and sufficiency of such notifications. The parties agree to use reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of force majeure events and to resume performance as soon as practicable.

During any period of force majeure, the obligations of the affected party shall be suspended to the extent directly impacted by the event. If the force majeure event continues for an extended period, typically considered to be thirty (30) days or more, either party may terminate the Agreement upon written notice without liability. Notably, the distinction between "User" and "Customer" is inconsistently applied in this section, potentially creating uncertainty as to which party may exercise termination rights under force majeure conditions.

It is further acknowledged that Tool_5’s obligation to maintain service availability, as described elsewhere in this Agreement, is subject to suspension during force majeure events. Nevertheless, conflicting language in the Service Availability section regarding notification and data integrity during service interruptions may lead to confusion concerning Tool_5’s responsibilities in such circumstances. Users and Customers are encouraged to communicate proactively to manage expectations and minimize disruptions.
