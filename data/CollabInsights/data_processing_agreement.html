Data Processing Agreement

Introduction

This Data Processing Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between the Data Controller and the Data Processor, collectively referred to as the "Parties," for the purpose of establishing the terms and conditions under which personal data will be processed in connection with the use of the CollabInsights platform ("Tool"). This Agreement is effective as of the date of acceptance or execution by both Parties and shall govern all data processing activities carried out through the Tool, ensuring compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other relevant regulations.

The purpose of this Agreement is to define the scope, nature, and purpose of data processing, specify the responsibilities and obligations of each Party, and establish safeguards to protect the rights and freedoms of data subjects. It reflects the Parties' mutual understanding that the Data Controller retains authority over the data and determines the purposes and means of processing, while the Data Processor processes data solely on the instructions of the Controller and for the purposes set forth herein.

Both Parties acknowledge the importance of safeguarding personal data against unauthorized access, loss, or disclosure, and agree to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure data security. This Agreement shall remain in effect for the duration of the data processing activities and shall continue to bind the Parties after termination or expiration of the underlying contractual relationship, insofar as data processing activities continue.

Purpose and Scope

This Agreement delineates the specific purposes for which personal data may be processed by the Data Processor in relation to the use of the CollabInsights platform. The primary purpose of data processing under this Agreement is to facilitate collaboration among data analysts by providing tools for sharing insights, visualizations, and real-time data project management. Data processing activities shall be strictly confined to these purposes and shall not extend to any other uses without prior written consent from the Data Controller.

The scope of this Agreement encompasses all personal data collected, stored, or otherwise processed through the CollabInsights platform. This includes, but is not limited to, user account information, project data, shared insights, and communication logs. The Data Processor shall process such data solely for the purpose of enabling the functionalities of the platform, including data analysis, visualization, and collaborative editing, and shall ensure that processing activities are limited to what is necessary to achieve these objectives.

Furthermore, the Agreement specifies that the Data Processor shall not process any personal data outside the instructions provided by the Data Controller, nor shall it process data for its own purposes or for any third parties. Any deviation from the specified scope must be authorized in writing by the Data Controller. This scope shall remain in effect for the duration of the contractual relationship, unless amended or terminated by mutual agreement in accordance with the terms herein.

Definitions

For the purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings specified below, unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:

  • "Data Controller": The natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data. In the context of this Agreement, the Data Controller is responsible for defining the objectives and scope of data collection and processing related to the use of the CollabInsights platform.
  • "Data Processor": The natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which processes personal data on behalf of the Data Controller. The Data Processor shall process data only according to the instructions of the Data Controller and in compliance with applicable data protection laws.
  • "Personal Data": Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ('data subject'); an identifiable person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, by reference to an identifier such as a name, an identification number, location data, or one or more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity of that person.
  • "Processing": Any operation or set of operations performed on personal data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.
  • "Data Subject": An individual who is the subject of personal data processed under this Agreement. Data subjects have rights regarding their personal data, including access, rectification, and erasure, which the Parties agree to uphold.
  • "Sub-Processor": Any third party engaged by the Data Processor to assist in processing personal data on behalf of the Data Controller, subject to the same contractual obligations to ensure data protection and confidentiality.
  • "Applicable Data Protection Laws": All relevant laws and regulations governing data protection and privacy applicable to the Parties, including but not limited to the GDPR, local data protection statutes, and any other relevant legislation.

Definitions provided herein shall be interpreted in accordance with the applicable legal context and shall apply uniformly throughout this Agreement unless explicitly stated otherwise.

Roles and Responsibilities

Each Party acknowledges and agrees that their respective roles and responsibilities are critical to ensuring compliance with applicable data protection laws and the effective execution of this Agreement. The Data Controller shall retain ultimate authority over the scope, purposes, and means of data processing related to the use of the CollabInsights platform. It shall be responsible for providing clear instructions to the Data Processor regarding the processing activities, including the types of personal data to be processed and the specific purposes for such processing.

The Data Processor shall process personal data solely in accordance with the documented instructions of the Data Controller, unless required to do so by law. The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure the security and confidentiality of the data, including but not limited to encryption, access controls, and regular security assessments. The Processor shall also assist the Data Controller in fulfilling data subjects' rights, such as access and rectification requests, and shall notify the Controller without undue delay of any data breaches or security incidents.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall ensure that all personnel involved in data processing are bound by confidentiality obligations and are adequately trained to handle personal data responsibly. It shall also conduct periodic audits to verify compliance with this Agreement and applicable laws. The Data Processor shall not engage any Sub-Processor without prior written approval from the Data Controller and shall ensure that any Sub-Processor complies with the same contractual obligations regarding data protection and confidentiality. Any breach of these responsibilities may result in contractual liability, including damages and termination of this Agreement.

Data Controller Obligations

The Data Controller shall ensure that all instructions provided to the Data Processor regarding the processing of personal data are clear, lawful, and in compliance with applicable data protection legislation. The Controller is responsible for ensuring that the collection, processing, and transfer of personal data are conducted in accordance with relevant legal requirements, including obtaining any necessary consents or legal grounds for processing such data.

It is incumbent upon the Data Controller to maintain accurate and up-to-date records of the personal data processed through the CollabInsights platform, including the purposes of processing and categories of data involved. The Controller shall also inform data subjects of their rights and provide them with the necessary information regarding data processing activities as mandated by applicable law. Additionally, the Controller shall cooperate with the Data Processor to facilitate data subject requests, such as access, rectification, or erasure, and shall ensure that such requests are handled promptly and in accordance with legal obligations.

The Data Controller shall implement appropriate measures to verify the lawful basis for processing personal data and shall regularly review processing activities to ensure ongoing compliance with applicable laws. In the event of a data breach or security incident, the Controller shall notify the relevant supervisory authority and affected data subjects without undue delay, in accordance with statutory requirements. Furthermore, the Controller shall provide the necessary support and guidance to the Data Processor to ensure that processing activities remain compliant with the terms of this Agreement and applicable legal standards.

Data Processor Obligations

The Data Processor shall process personal data exclusively in accordance with the documented instructions received from the Data Controller and shall not process such data for any purpose outside the scope of this Agreement unless expressly authorized in writing by the Data Controller. The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including measures to prevent unauthorized access, accidental loss, destruction, or alteration of personal data. These measures shall include, but are not limited to, encryption, access controls, secure storage, and regular security assessments.

The Processor shall ensure that all personnel involved in data processing are bound by confidentiality obligations and have received adequate training on data protection obligations. The Processor shall assist the Data Controller in fulfilling data subjects’ rights, such as access, rectification, and erasure requests, within the timeframes stipulated by applicable laws. In the event of a data breach or security incident, the Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay and cooperate fully to mitigate any potential harm and fulfill statutory reporting requirements.

Furthermore, the Processor shall not engage any Sub-Processor without prior written approval from the Data Controller and shall ensure that any Sub-Processor engaged complies with the same obligations regarding data protection, confidentiality, and security as set out herein. The Processor shall maintain a record of all processing activities carried out on behalf of the Data Controller and shall make such records available to the Controller upon request for audit purposes. The Processor shall cooperate with the Data Controller in audits, inspections, and investigations to verify compliance with this Agreement and applicable data protection laws.

Joint Controller Provisions

In circumstances where the Parties concurrently determine the purposes and means of processing personal data related to the use of the CollabInsights platform, they shall be considered joint controllers under applicable data protection laws. In such cases, the Parties agree to allocate their respective responsibilities in a transparent manner, ensuring compliance with legal requirements for joint controllership. The Parties shall establish and maintain a clear, written arrangement that specifies their respective roles, responsibilities, and obligations regarding data processing activities, including but not limited to data subject rights, data breach notifications, and data protection impact assessments.

Both Parties shall cooperate actively to ensure lawful processing and to uphold the rights of data subjects. This cooperation includes providing each other with necessary information, coordinating responses to data subject requests, and jointly ensuring that processing operations are carried out in accordance with applicable legal standards. Each Party shall be responsible for ensuring that their processing activities are lawful, and they shall inform each other promptly of any incidents or issues that may impact compliance.

In the event of a data breach or security incident affecting shared personal data, the Parties shall notify each other without undue delay and collaborate to mitigate potential harm. They shall also coordinate efforts to notify supervisory authorities and affected data subjects, as required by law. This joint controller arrangement shall remain in effect until such time as the Parties agree to amend or terminate it in writing, with all obligations regarding data processing clearly delineated to prevent overlapping or conflicting responsibilities.

Data Handling and Processing

The Data Processor shall handle and process personal data strictly in accordance with the instructions provided by the Data Controller, ensuring that all processing activities are lawful, fair, and transparent. The Processor shall only process data necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Agreement and shall avoid any extraneous or unauthorized processing activities. All data handling procedures shall incorporate appropriate technical and organizational measures to safeguard the integrity, confidentiality, and security of the personal data throughout the processing lifecycle.

In performing its duties, the Data Processor shall implement measures such as data minimization, pseudonymization where applicable, and secure data storage to mitigate risks related to unauthorized access, accidental loss, or destruction. The Processor shall ensure that processing is conducted only within the scope of the documented instructions and shall refrain from using the data for any purposes not expressly authorized by the Data Controller. Any incidental processing outside the scope must be immediately reported and authorized prior to execution.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall maintain detailed records of processing activities, including the nature, scope, and duration of processing, as well as the categories of data and data subjects involved. These records shall be made available to the Data Controller upon request and shall support compliance with applicable data protection laws. The Processor shall also cooperate fully with audits, inspections, or investigations conducted by the Data Controller or relevant authorities to verify adherence to this Agreement and legal standards. Data transfer outside the relevant jurisdiction shall only occur if expressly authorized by the Data Controller and in compliance with applicable cross-border data transfer regulations.

Processing Instructions

The Data Processor shall process personal data solely in accordance with the documented instructions provided by the Data Controller. These instructions shall specify the purposes for processing, the types of personal data involved, and any specific processing procedures to be followed. The Processor shall not process personal data for any other purpose or manner than explicitly instructed by the Controller, unless required by applicable law, in which case the Processor shall notify the Controller prior to such processing, unless prohibited by law from doing so.

In carrying out processing activities, the Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure data security and protect against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction of personal data. Such measures shall include, but are not limited to, encryption, access controls, and secure storage protocols. The Processor shall ensure that all personnel involved in data processing are bound by confidentiality obligations and are adequately trained to handle personal data in accordance with applicable legal standards.

Any modification or addition to the processing instructions shall require prior written approval from the Data Controller. The Processor shall maintain a record of all processing instructions received and executed and shall provide such documentation to the Controller upon request. This obligation ensures transparency and accountability in processing activities and facilitates compliance with applicable data protection legislation. The Processor shall also cooperate with the Controller to facilitate data subject rights and to respond to any requests or inquiries related to processing activities, ensuring that such activities remain within the scope of the initial instructions and legal compliance obligations.

Retention and Deletion

The Data Processor shall retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes specified in this Agreement or to comply with applicable legal, regulatory, or contractual obligations. Upon reaching the end of the retention period, or upon termination of this Agreement, the Data Processor shall securely delete or anonymize all personal data processed on behalf of the Data Controller, unless otherwise instructed by the Controller or required by law to retain such data for a longer period.

In determining the appropriate retention periods, the Parties shall consider the nature of the data, the purposes of processing, and any legal or regulatory requirements. The Data Processor shall maintain accurate records of data deletion activities, including the date and method of deletion, and shall provide such documentation to the Data Controller upon request. The Data Processor shall implement secure deletion procedures that ensure personal data is irreversibly destroyed, preventing any potential re-identification.

In cases where personal data is subject to ongoing legal or contractual obligations, the Data Controller shall notify the Data Processor of any specific retention requirements. The Data Processor shall then retain the relevant data only for the duration necessary to meet those obligations. Any personal data that is no longer required shall be promptly deleted or rendered pseudonymous, in accordance with best practices for data security. The Parties agree that retention and deletion activities shall be conducted in compliance with all applicable data protection laws, ensuring the continued protection of data subjects' rights throughout the data lifecycle.

Sub-Processors

The Data Processor shall not engage any Sub-Processor to carry out processing activities on behalf of the Data Controller without obtaining prior written authorization from the Data Controller. When engaging a Sub-Processor, the Data Processor shall ensure that the Sub-Processor is bound by written contractual obligations that provide at least the same level of data protection, confidentiality, and security as those set forth in this Agreement. Such contractual obligations shall include provisions requiring the Sub-Processor to process personal data only on documented instructions from the Data Processor, implement appropriate technical and organizational measures, and assist the Data Processor in fulfilling its obligations regarding data subject rights and security incidents.

The Data Processor shall maintain a current list of all Sub-Processors engaged and shall provide this list to the Data Controller upon request. The Data Controller reserves the right to object to the appointment of any Sub-Processor and may require the Data Processor to replace or remove a Sub-Processor if deemed necessary to ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws and this Agreement. The Data Processor shall remain fully liable for the acts and omissions of any Sub-Processor engaged pursuant to this section as if such acts or omissions were those of the Data Processor itself.

In the event of a breach or failure by a Sub-Processor to fulfill its contractual obligations, the Data Processor shall remain responsible for ensuring that all processing activities comply with the terms of this Agreement and applicable law. The engagement of Sub-Processors shall be documented in writing, and the Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller of any new Sub-Processor engagements prior to such processing commencing. This process ensures transparency and maintains the integrity of data protection obligations throughout the processing chain.

Security Measures

The Data Processor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk associated with the processing of personal data. Such measures shall include, but are not limited to, encryption of data both in transit and at rest, access controls based on the principle of least privilege, secure storage, and regular vulnerability assessments. The Processor shall ensure that all personnel involved in processing activities are trained in data protection principles and are bound by confidentiality obligations.

In addition, the Processor shall establish procedures for detecting, reporting, and investigating security incidents, including data breaches, in accordance with applicable laws and industry standards. The Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay upon becoming aware of a security incident affecting personal data, providing sufficient information to assess the impact, remedial actions taken, and steps for mitigation. The Processor shall cooperate fully with the Data Controller and relevant authorities in any investigation or remedial actions concerning such incidents.

Periodic review and testing of security measures shall be conducted to verify their effectiveness and to adapt to evolving threats. The Processor shall ensure that any sub-processors engaged are subject to equivalent security obligations, and shall require them to implement measures consistent with those outlined herein. The Parties acknowledge that security is a continuous process and commit to maintaining and updating their security protocols to safeguard personal data throughout the duration of processing activities.

Technical and Organizational Measures

The Data Processor shall implement comprehensive technical and organizational measures to ensure the security and confidentiality of personal data processed under this Agreement. These measures shall be appropriate to the nature, scope, context, and purpose of processing, as well as the risks posed to data subjects' rights and freedoms. Such measures shall include, but are not limited to, encryption of personal data both in transit and at rest, multi-factor authentication for access controls, and regular vulnerability assessments to identify and mitigate potential security weaknesses.

The Data Processor shall establish procedures for maintaining data integrity and ensuring the availability of personal data, including regular data backups and disaster recovery plans. Access to personal data shall be restricted to authorized personnel based on the principle of least privilege, with access rights regularly reviewed and updated. Additionally, the Data Processor shall provide ongoing training to personnel involved in data processing to foster awareness of security obligations and data protection best practices.

Periodic audits and testing of security controls shall be conducted to verify their effectiveness, and findings shall be documented and addressed promptly. The Data Processor shall also ensure that any engaged Sub-Processors adhere to equivalent security standards, incorporating contractual provisions requiring compliance with these measures. In the event of a security incident or data breach, the Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller immediately and cooperate fully in investigating, mitigating, and reporting the incident to relevant authorities, as required by applicable laws.

Incident Response and Notification

In the event of a personal data breach or security incident affecting the data processed under this Agreement, the Data Processor shall promptly initiate its incident response procedures to contain, investigate, and remediate the incident. The Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay, and where feasible, no later than 24 hours after becoming aware of the breach, providing sufficient details regarding the nature, scope, and potential impact of the incident. Such notification shall include, but is not limited to, the types of data affected, the circumstances of the breach, and the measures taken or planned to mitigate any adverse effects.

The Data Processor shall cooperate fully with the Data Controller in assessing the incident's severity and shall assist in fulfilling any legal, regulatory, or contractual obligations related to breach notifications, including notification to supervisory authorities and affected data subjects when required by applicable laws. The Processor shall also document all actions taken in response to the incident, including containment, investigation, and remediation efforts, and shall preserve relevant evidence for potential legal or audit purposes. To ensure effective response, the Parties shall review and update incident response procedures periodically.

It is understood that ambiguity may exist regarding the threshold for notification and the scope of information required; therefore, the Data Processor shall act in good faith and provide clear, comprehensive reports to facilitate timely compliance. Failure to notify in accordance with this section may result in contractual liabilities, including damages, and potential regulatory penalties. Both Parties agree to maintain open communication during incident handling to minimize harm and ensure compliance with all applicable data protection regulations.

Compliance with Applicable Laws

Both Parties shall ensure that all processing activities conducted under this Agreement comply with all relevant and applicable data protection laws and regulations, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), national data privacy statutes, and any other jurisdiction-specific legal requirements. The Data Controller shall be responsible for establishing the lawful basis for processing personal data and for providing clear, transparent notices to data subjects regarding the nature and purpose of data collection and processing activities.

The Data Processor shall process personal data solely in accordance with the documented instructions of the Data Controller and shall implement appropriate measures to ensure compliance with applicable legal standards. This includes facilitating data subject rights such as access, rectification, or erasure, and cooperating with the Controller in conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) when required.

In the event of a legal change impacting data processing obligations, the Parties shall review and amend this Agreement accordingly to maintain compliance. The Data Processor shall promptly inform the Data Controller of any legal obligations that may require processing activities outside the scope of initial instructions, and shall seek prior approval before implementing any such activities. Failure to adhere to applicable legal requirements shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement and may result in contractual remedies, including termination, damages, or sanctions, in addition to any responsibilities under applicable law. Each Party shall bear its own costs associated with ensuring compliance and shall cooperate fully to address any legal or regulatory issues arising from processing activities under this Agreement.

Data Protection Regulations

Both Parties acknowledge and agree that their respective obligations under this Agreement shall be governed by and comply with all applicable data protection regulations, including the European Union’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and any other relevant national or regional laws. The Data Controller shall ensure that lawful grounds for processing personal data are established in accordance with applicable legal standards, such as obtaining necessary consents or demonstrating legitimate interests where appropriate.

The Data Processor shall implement measures to uphold the principles of data minimization, purpose limitation, and data accuracy, and shall process personal data in a manner that ensures its security and confidentiality. Both Parties shall cooperate to facilitate compliance with legal obligations related to data subject rights, data breach notifications, and data impact assessments. In particular, the Parties shall maintain records of processing activities as required by applicable regulations and shall cooperate in audits or investigations conducted by supervisory authorities.

Failure to comply with these regulations shall constitute a material breach and may result in contractual remedies, including termination and damages, as well as potential regulatory sanctions.

Cross-Border Data Transfers

In the event that personal data processed under this Agreement is transferred outside the jurisdiction where it was originally collected, the Parties shall ensure that such cross-border data transfers are conducted in strict compliance with applicable data protection laws and regulations. The Data Controller shall be responsible for verifying that appropriate safeguards are in place prior to transferring any personal data to a third country or international organization. Acceptable transfer mechanisms include, but are not limited to, the use of Standard Contractual Clauses approved by relevant supervisory authorities, binding corporate rules, or an adequacy decision issued by the European Commission or applicable regulatory body.

The Data Processor shall not transfer personal data outside the scope of this Agreement unless explicitly authorized by the Data Controller and in accordance with the applicable legal requirements. Prior to any transfer, the Processor shall conduct a transfer impact assessment to verify that the recipient jurisdiction provides an adequate level of data protection and shall document the basis for such assessment. Additionally, the Processor shall ensure that any data transferred is subject to encryption and other appropriate security measures to mitigate risks associated with cross-border data flows.

Both Parties agree to cooperate and provide necessary documentation to demonstrate compliance with cross-border transfer obligations. Any deviation from approved transfer mechanisms shall be considered a breach of this Agreement and may lead to contractual remedies, including termination and liability for damages. These provisions shall remain in effect for the duration of the processing activities, and the Parties shall review and update their transfer arrangements periodically to ensure ongoing compliance with evolving legal standards and requirements.

The Data Processor shall be responsible for implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures to prevent unauthorized access, accidental loss, or destruction of data and shall be liable for any breach resulting from inadequate safeguards. Conversely, the Data Controller shall be liable for any damages resulting from incorrect or unlawful instructions that cause processing violations.

In the event of a breach of this Agreement or applicable legal obligations, the responsible Party shall indemnify and hold harmless the other Party from and against any claims, damages, fines, or penalties incurred as a direct result of such breach. This includes, but is not limited to, damages arising from data breaches, unauthorized disclosures, or failure to comply with applicable data protection regulations. The indemnifying Party shall cover all reasonable costs, including legal fees and regulatory fines, associated with defending claims or investigations.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, neither Party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages, including lost profits or business interruption, except in cases of gross negligence or willful misconduct. The Parties agree that the total liability arising out of or related to this Agreement shall not exceed the aggregate amount paid or payable under the governing contractual arrangement during the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, unless otherwise specified herein.

Both Parties acknowledge that this liability and indemnification framework reflects a fair allocation of risks based on their respective roles and responsibilities. It is understood that each Party shall maintain appropriate insurance coverage to mitigate potential liabilities arising from processing activities under this Agreement. Any limitations or exclusions of liability shall be explicitly stated in the relevant contractual provisions and shall not apply to damages resulting from intentional misconduct or statutory obligations to provide remedies.

Termination

This Agreement may be terminated by either Party upon written notice to the other Party if there is a material breach of any provision herein that remains unremedied after thirty (30) days following receipt of written notice specifying the breach. Additionally, either Party may terminate this Agreement without cause by providing at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to the other Party. In the event of termination, the Data Processor shall, at the choice of the Data Controller, either securely delete or return all personal data processed under this Agreement, including copies and backups, unless retention is required by applicable law.

Upon termination, the Data Processor shall cooperate fully with the Data Controller to ensure an orderly transition of data processing activities and to facilitate the secure transfer or deletion of all relevant data. The obligations related to confidentiality, data security, and liability shall survive termination to the extent necessary to protect the rights of data subjects and to address ongoing legal or contractual obligations.

Furthermore, any provisions that by their nature should survive termination, including but not limited to confidentiality, liability, indemnification, and data protection obligations, shall remain in effect indefinitely. The Parties agree that termination shall not affect accrued rights or obligations, nor shall it relieve either Party from liability arising from prior breaches of this Agreement. This section aims to ensure a clear, fair conclusion of the contractual relationship while safeguarding the integrity and security of personal data post-termination.

Effects of Termination

Upon termination of this Agreement, the Data Processor shall immediately cease all processing activities relating to personal data unless otherwise instructed by the Data Controller. The Processor shall, at the discretion of the Data Controller, either securely delete or return all personal data, including copies and backups, in a manner compliant with applicable data protection laws and industry best practices. If the Data Controller requests the return of data, the Data Processor shall do so within a reasonable timeframe, ensuring data integrity and security throughout the transfer process.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Data Processor shall retain any personal data necessary to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, or enforce rights under this Agreement or applicable law. Such retention shall be limited to the minimum period required and shall be subject to confidentiality and security obligations. Additionally, the Data Processor shall provide the Data Controller with all necessary documentation to confirm the deletion or return of data, including audit logs and certificates of destruction if applicable.

All confidentiality, security, and indemnity obligations shall survive the termination of this Agreement for any personal data processed during the term. The Parties acknowledge that data processed for legitimate legal or regulatory purposes may be retained for a longer period, subject to applicable laws and internal policies. This section ensures that, following termination, personal data is handled in a manner that protects the rights of data subjects and maintains compliance with legal standards, thereby preventing unauthorized use or disclosure post-termination.

Post-Termination Obligations

Following the termination of this Agreement, the Data Processor shall, within a timeframe specified by the Data Controller, securely delete or return all personal data processed on behalf of the Data Controller, including copies, backups, and any other derivative forms, unless retention is legally mandated. The Data Processor shall provide written confirmation to the Data Controller that such data has been irreversibly destroyed or properly transferred, along with evidence of compliance, such as certificates of destruction or detailed audit logs.

During this process, the Data Processor shall ensure that all data handling complies with applicable data protection laws and industry standards for secure data deletion. The Data Processor shall also cooperate fully with the Data Controller to address any ongoing legal or regulatory obligations, including assisting with data subject requests related to the data processed during the term of the Agreement. Any residual data necessary for legal, audit, or dispute resolution purposes shall be retained under strict confidentiality and security measures, and shall be deleted once the lawful retention period has expired.

Furthermore, the Data Processor shall retain all documentation, records, and evidence related to the processing activities, including those pertaining to data deletion or return, for a period required under applicable laws or as specified in this Agreement. Both Parties agree to maintain confidentiality obligations concerning all data processed and processed records even after the termination of this Agreement, to protect the rights of data subjects and ensure ongoing compliance with data protection standards.

Failure to comply with post-termination data handling obligations shall constitute a material breach and may result in legal liabilities, including damages and sanctions. The Parties shall also ensure that any contractual or legal obligations related to ongoing monitoring, audit, or reporting are fulfilled to maintain transparency and accountability after the conclusion of this Agreement.
